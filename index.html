Carpet Cleaning

Pick the condition. Follow the mix. Don't skip the rinse.

Method

  3. 3Agitate with CRB
  4. 4Wand cleaning — 1–2 steam passes with 2–3 dry passes
  5. 5Air mover × 2
  6. 6Groom

Really Dirty
Higher foot traffic. 1+ year since pro clean.
Method

  2. 2Yellow tip · 7 scoops Black Label · 2 scoops GM
  3. 31 scoop O2 (for lighter carpets)
  4. 48 oz citrus solvent — if grease, deep-set soil, or 3+ years uncleaned
  5. 5Agitate with CRB
  6. 6Wand cleaning — 3+ steam passes followed by 4–5 dry passes
  7. 7Air mover × 2
  8. 8Groom
Rinse: Fiber Neutralizer · 4 oz per 2 gal · red sprayer
3
Level 3 · Restoration
Severely Dirty
Extreme wear. 5+ years uncleaned. Aggressive method.
Method
  1. 1Vacuum if needed ($5 per room)
  2. 2Yellow tip OUT — 1:1 dilution rate
  3. 37 scoops Frog Nasty into Hydroforce with hot water — mix VERY WELL
  4. 4Wait 10 minutes after application
  5. 5Agitate with CRB on traffic areas. Clean edges with wand.
  6. 6Wand cleaning — 3 steam strokes with 4–5 dry strokes (extra dry strokes if needed)
  7. 7Air mover × 2
  8. 8Groom
Rinse: Fiber Neutralizer · 4 oz per 2 gal · red sprayer
Booster options: Citrus solvent — 8 oz per FULL Hydroforce. O2 helps with white or light-colored carpets. Grout Master can be added — 2–4 scoops per full Hydroforce.

Condo Carpets

Method
  1. 1Vacuum (if needed) — vacuum again, sand is embedded
  2. 2Yellow tip · 7 scoops 10K into Hydroforce, mix well
  3. 3Agitate with CRB
  4. 4Wand cleaning — use low PSI wand

Understanding Carpet Dirt Levels

A quick read on what the customer's carpet is actually telling you.

1
Regularly Soiled · Normal Dirty
Standard day-to-day accumulation. Vacuumed at least weekly and professionally cleaned every 6–12 months. This is routine maintenance.
2
Heavily Soiled · Really Dirty
Higher-than-normal foot traffic, or hasn't been professionally cleaned in over a year. Vacuuming may be inconsistent. Visible dirt buildup and traffic-area wear are common.
3
Severely Soiled · Extreme Cases
Not cleaned in 5+ years, extreme traffic wear, poor or nonexistent vacuuming. Buildup includes deep-set grime, odors, and staining — often requires aggressive restoration techniques.

Dirty Carpet Edges (Filtration Soiling)

Dark lines along walls or under doors? That's filtration soiling — dirty air moving through gaps and getting trapped in the carpet fibers. Usually linked to the HVAC system.

If routine cleaning doesn't resolve it, a specialized Filter Edge Treatment may be needed. Pricing varies by severity. Recommend regular HVAC filter changes to prevent it returning.
Modules / Textile ID

Textile Identification

IICRC S100 / S300 fiber ID — visual, burn, and bleach tests.
Why this matters
Wrong chemistry on the wrong fiber = damage, complaint, claim. IICRC standards require fiber ID before cleaning. Always pull samples from a hidden area — under cushions, inside seams, behind furniture, or rug fringe.

Visual & Tactile Pre-Check

First pass before any chemical work. Look and feel.

  • Sheen — natural fibers are usually matte. Synthetics often have a sheen.
  • Hand feel — wool is springy and warm. Cotton soft and cool. Polyester smooth and slick. Olefin waxy. Silk fine and slippery.
  • Construction — jute backing or hand-knotted = natural. Tufted with synthetic backing = synthetic.
  • Fiber length — short staple suggests wool or cotton. Long filament suggests synthetic.

Chlorine Bleach Solubility Test

Use this when the burn test points to natural protein and you need to confirm wool before locking in your chemistry.

Procedure
  1. 1Pull a small fiber sample from a hidden area
  2. 2Drop into a small container of full-strength liquid chlorine bleach
  3. 3Wait 15–20 minutes
  4. 4Read the result below

Reading the Result

After 15–20 minutes, look at what's left in the container.

✓
Wool / Silk DISSOLVES COMPLETELY
Bleach destroys protein bonds. Treat as wool. pH 5–8 only, no chlorine, no high heat, gentle agitation, Fiber Neutralizer rinse.
—
Cotton / Cellulose STAYS INTACT
Stays intact, may discolor. Not protein.
—
Synthetics NO CHANGE
Stays intact, no change. Confirm specific synthetic via burn test.
Safety: Wear gloves. Work in ventilation. Don't mix bleach with ammonia or acid cleaners.

Once you know the fiber, here's what to do — and what not to do.

W

Wool / Silk

  • pH 5–8 only — neutral to slightly acidic
  • Never high-alkaline detergents
  • Never chlorine bleach
  • Never hot water
  • Fiber Neutralizer rinse · air dry
C

Cotton / Linen

  • Watch for cellulose browning
  • Dry quickly to prevent wicking
  • Browning treatment if needed
N

Nylon

  • Most durable — wider pH range
  • Standard protocols apply
  • Stain-resistant treatments may not tolerate high pH
P

Polyester

  • Oleophilic — loves oils
  • Citrus solvent & oil-cutting boosters work well
  • Standard protocols
O

Olefin

  • Bleach-stable
  • Heat-sensitive — never high heat
  • Citrus solvent essential for grease
  • Common in Berber and outdoor
A

Acrylic

  • Heat-sensitive — keep temps moderate
  • Standard pH range OK
EVERY TEXTILE JOB
Always
  • Colorfastness check before any cleaner — white towel blot test
  • Document fiber ID in the job notes
  • When in doubt, treat it as the most delicate fiber it could be
  • IICRC S100 and S300 require fiber ID — your protection in a damage claim
Modules / Spotting

Spotting Guide

Bridgepoint Spot & Stain Removal Kit · 5 categories.
Before you start
Pretest in a hidden area first. Work outside-in to prevent spreading. Don't over-apply. Tamp, don't scrub. Finish every job with Spot Stop to prevent wicking and re-soiling.
1
Synthetic Color & Dye
Kool-Aid · children's red medicine · fruit punch · sports drinks
Procedure
  1. 1Apply Red Zone Ready — enough to thoroughly wet the stain but not soak the backing
  2. 2Lay wet cotton towel (usually double thickness) over stain. Apply heated iron at lowest steam setting. Quick check every 20–30 seconds
  3. 3When color is gone, a yellow cast usually remains. Rinse thoroughly
  4. 4Mist Spot Stop and work in with the Whiz Groom
2
Organic Stains & Natural Dyes
Urine · decaffeinated coffee · mold · wood stains
Procedure
  1. 1Apply Stain Zone to contaminated area
  2. 2Lightly agitate and allow to dwell until stain is gone
  3. 3If stain is gone, extract rinse. Otherwise Stain Zone self-neutralizes with no re-soiling and continues working until it dries
3
Oil, Resin or Pigmented
Ink · grease · cosmetics · adhesives · polish · gum
Ink & Marker
  1. 1Apply Avenge Pro directly to stain, agitate, dwell 30 seconds
  2. 2Dry extract with vac tool, repeat if needed
  3. 3Apply All Solv Extreme · agitate · then Avenge Pro · agitate
  4. 4Rinse and mist with Spot Stop
Fingernail Polish

Critical containment trick: apply Avenge Pro AROUND the stain first. It blocks All Solv Extreme and dissolved pigment from wicking into the backing. Avenge Pro doesn't "set" stains — it's safe to lead with.

  1. 1Apply Avenge Pro around the contaminated area (not on it) to guard against wicking
  2. 2Apply All Solv Extreme, lightly agitate, absorb pigment into a white towel. Repeat until most pigment is gone
  3. 3Apply Avenge Pro and agitate lightly
  4. 4Rinse and mist Spot Stop, work in with Whiz Groom
Gum (chewing)
  1. 1Poke holes in gum surface with the Gum Getter
  2. 2Apply Gel Break to surface, dwell 5–10 minutes
  3. 3Agitate and remove gum with the Gum Getter into a towel. Repeat as needed. For residue: apply small amount of Gel Break, agitate with towel, then rinse thoroughly with water
  4. 4Mist Spot Stop and work in with Whiz Groom
4
Protein & Most Food Spills
Blood · egg · chocolate sauce · most food
Procedure
  1. 1Remove excess contaminant (blot, scrape, vacuum)
  2. 2Apply Avenge Pro and agitate. If hard, allow Avenge Pro to dwell and soften the contaminant
  3. 3Rinse and repeat if necessary
  4. 4Mist Spot Stop and work in with Whiz Groom
Note: Some food stains contain synthetic or organic dyes. If stain remains after the above, vacuum dry and follow Cat 1 (Red Zone Ready) for synthetic dyes or Cat 2 (Stain Zone) for organic dyes.
5
Specialty Stains
Rust · yellowing · water stains
Procedure
  1. 1Apply T-Rust
  2. 2Lightly agitate and allow to dwell until stain disappears
  3. 3Rinse thoroughly
Note: Some yellowing and water stains may also respond to Stain Zone. For unknown specialty stains, see the Unknown tab.

Unknown Stain Procedure

When the stain origin is unknown, work the kit in this order. Avenge Pro doesn't "set" stains — it's always safe to lead with.

1
Apply Avenge Pro · agitate
If stain is releasing → continue and rinse
2
If stain remains: Apply All Solv Extreme · agitate
If releasing → continue, then apply Avenge Pro, rinse
3
If stain remains: Apply Stain Zone OR Red Zone Ready
Origin is unknown → test in a hidden area to determine which works
EVERY SPOT JOB
Always
  • Pretest any solution in a hidden area before using on the visible stain
  • Remove excess (blot, scrape, vacuum) before applying liquid
  • Work from outside edges to center to prevent spreading
  • Tamp, don't scrub. Aggressive scrubbing causes texture change ("fuzzy carpet")
  • Don't over-apply — too much chemical spreads the stain and damages backing
  • Be patient. Most spotters need dwell time. Let chemistry do the work
  • Finish every job with Spot Stop — encapsulates residue, prevents re-soiling

The 7 chemicals and the agitation tools in the Bridgepoint Pro Kit.

Avenge Pro

Universal · Water-based

The most versatile spotter. Lead with this on Cat 3, 4, and Unknown. Won't "set" stains — safe to use first on virtually anything organic, oil-based, or food.

All Solv Extreme

Gelled solvent

Used in Cat 3 (oils, resins, polish, ink). Always after Avenge Pro — Avenge Pro contains the spread, then All Solv lifts the pigment.

Gel Break

Gum & adhesive solvent

Specialty solvent for gum, adhesives, and sticky residue. 5–10 minute dwell. Used with the Gum Getter tool.

Stain Zone

Organic stain remover · Cat 2

For organic stains and natural dyes (urine, coffee, mold, wood). Self-neutralizes on the carpet — no re-soiling, keeps working until it dries.

Red Zone Ready

Synthetic dye remover · Cat 1

For synthetic dye stains (Kool-Aid, fruit punch, red medicine). Requires heat — apply through wet cotton towel with a heated iron. Yellow cast usually remains; rinse thoroughly.

T-Rust

Specialty · Cat 5

Acidic rust remover. Apply, dwell until stain disappears, rinse thoroughly.

Spot Stop

Encapsulating · Anti-wicking

The final step on every job. Encapsulates residue, drives it under the fiber, prevents re-soiling and wicking. Misted on, worked in with the Whiz Groom.
